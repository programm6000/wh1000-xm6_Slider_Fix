# wh1000-xm6_Slider_Fix
The Sliders inside the Sony WH-1000XM6 headphones can break under normal use due to the plastic limiters snapping, mine broke while I was lying in bed. If this happens you will find that the earcup on whichever side broke will simply fall out. Pushing it in again will neither help nor cause further damage (assuming no excessive force is used) as the limiters have snapped, they cannot be snapped back in. When i checked the price of a replacement piece the only thing i found was the entire headband for an extortionate 180 USD. Seeing this I decided instead to mix it myself. The following document is instructions on how to replace the snapped piece. 

You will need: Tweezers, a set of (very) small screwdriver bits, superglue, Access to a 3D printer, PLA fillament is fine and what I used, if you do not own one most people who do are generally very happy to print you something on theirs, a small file such as the type you might find on a swiss army knife, and patience. I also recommend you have a contianer for all the screws that you remove when taking the headphones apart as well as a pair of disposable rubber gloves as the 3D print that you will have to superglue is rather difficult to properly glue without touching the parts that have glue on them, and some masking tape to help with holding the cushioning on the headband where it should be where reassembling. 

THIS GUIDE IS NOT PERFECT, IF YOU FOLLOW IT YOU SHOULD BE COMFORTABLE OPENING ELECTRONICS AND KNOW HOW NOT TO CAUSE DAMAGE TO ANY INTERNAL COMPONENTS. 

Please read all steps before starting to ensure you are comfortable following them all. 

1. Clear a workspace and make sure you have a good light source.

2. Follow the disassembly instrucions in this video https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zRyc5aU8pZ0 until you can see the metal plate holding the headband to the earcups. You do not need to take the individual boards or the battery out. 

3. Unscrew the metal plate holding the headband to the earcups, unplug the calbe coming from the opening from its socket and gently remove the end of the headband from the earcup. At the beginning of the padded part of the headband there is a plastic ring covering the end of the cloth. Gently pull this off so that the cloth ends beneath are accessible. 

4. Using a small screwdriver bit, slowly peel the cloth from beneath where the plastic ring was. You should encounter some adhesive as you do this. is possible, gently remove it.

5. Carefully push the now freed end on the cloth over the rest of the headband until it is slightly over halfway to the other end. 

6. Carefully loosed the foam padding on the end of the headband that you are repairing on the underneath side (relative to when wearing the headphones and standing upright) until you see 3 screws, undo these. 

7. You can now pull open off the black plastic panel the screws were holding down. Inside you will find the rails that the original slider used. 

8. On the end of the part of the headband that did the  sliding you should see a small screw with a star shaped head, unscrew this and remove remove the black plastic piece from it's place. This is the piece that failed. 

9. Take your file and very carefully file through this piece until you have reached the calbe. Make absolutely sure that you do not puncture the cable housing, which is also black, as you otherwise risk damage to the data lines inside. Once you have reached the Cable pull it out.

10. Slice and print sonyWF-1000XM6HeadStrapSliderStopPieceReplacement.step. I recommend using the finest settings your printer has.

11. Attempt to connect the two halves of the replacement piece. If they do not fit properly this is probably due to overhang on the top tooth and bottom grove. File excess away as best you can until it fits.

12. Place the cable inside the two parts and close them. They should be able to fit into the previously mentioned rails now.

13. If you have any, put your gloves on and glue the parts together with the cable in them making sure that the part of the calbe in question matches the spot that the original piece was attached to. 

14. Leave until fully dry. Wet glue on the rails may cause jams once assembled. 

15. Place into rails,0872261 then place replacement part where original part was and screw in.

16. Close panel and screw down. 

17. Reassemble. You can follow steps 2. - 7. in reverse if unsure how.

18. Press power button.

Your newly installed slider has slightly less resistance than the original on the rails so it will move before the side that did not break. 
I also strongly recommend that you treat the headphones with extra care as the new 3D printed part is unlikely to be any stronger than the original and removing the replacement part that has been glued in place will probably prove very difficult.
